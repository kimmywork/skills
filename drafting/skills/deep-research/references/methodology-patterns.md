# Methodology Patterns

Choose one when the investigation requires more than quick source tracing.

## Systematic review

Use for literature or evidence landscape questions. Define inclusion/exclusion criteria, search strategy, screening log, and synthesis table.

## Comparative case study

Use when comparing products, policies, organizations, designs, or incidents. Define comparison dimensions before collecting evidence.

## Root-cause analysis

Use for failures. Trace timeline, symptoms, proximate cause, contributing factors, missing controls, and prevention.

## System mapping

Use for codebases, organizations, workflows, or ecosystems. Map components, data flow, dependencies, ownership, and failure points.

## Market or ecosystem scan

Use for competitive or landscape research. Track actors, capabilities, positioning, evidence, and uncertainty.

## Research brief

For lightweight work: question, method, findings, evidence table, confidence, gaps, next steps.
